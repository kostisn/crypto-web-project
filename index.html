Introduction

But first, what is blockchain? At its core, blockchain is a decentralized, digital ledger that records transactions across a network of computers. Each block in the chain contains a number of transactions, and every block is linked to the one before it, creating an unbroken chain of records. This technology is the backbone of many cryptocurrencies such as Bitcoin, Ethereum, and Litecoin, but its potential extends far beyond just digital currencies.

Some key features of blockchain technology

Decentralized:
A blockchain is decentralized, meaning it is not controlled by a single entity or organization. This makes it resistant to censorship and tampering.

Secure:
Transactions on a blockchain are secured using cryptography, making it difficult for anyone to alter the data once it has been recorded.

Transparent:
All transactions on a blockchain are visible to everyone on the network, making it a transparent way to track and verify information.

Immutable:
Once a transaction has been recorded on a blockchain, it cannot be altered or deleted. This makes it a tamper-proof way to store and transfer data.
